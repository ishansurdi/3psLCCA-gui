Road Roughness

As per IRC-SP:16-2019 and IRC-SP:30-2019:

As per Clause 2.1 of IRC-SP:16-2019, road roughness is the result of either built-in irregularities due to construction defects/deficiencies or consolidation/displacement of materials within the pavement structure due to traffic movement. Road roughness consists of deviations of the pavement surface from a true planar surface with characteristic dimensions that affect vehicle dynamics, ride quality, dynamic loads, and drainage (e.g., longitudinal profile, transverse profile, and cross slope; ASTM E867).

As per Clause 6.5 of IRC-SP:30-2019, road roughness (RG) is measured by the British Towed Fifth Wheel Bump Integrator (BI) and expressed in mm/km. Recommended values on Indian roads can be found in Tables 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3 of IRC:SP:16-2019.

Another method of measuring roughness is the International Roughness Index (IRI). The following equation presents the relationship between BI (in mm/km) and IRI (in m/km):

BI=630×(IRI)1.12BI = 630 \times (IRI)^{1.12}
